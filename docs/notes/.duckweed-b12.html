1. 100g of fresh duckweed has been found to contain 18mcg of vitamin B12. The company was growing lemna minor and wolffia, seemingly in outdoor tanks exposed to the air.

  2. Public Health England recommends 1.5mcg of vitamin B12 per day, or the amount in 8g of fresh duckweed.

  3. Dried duckweed should be about 10% the weight of fresh, and B12 seems resilient to drying.

  4. Therefore, a gram or two of dried duckweed would contain enough B12.
