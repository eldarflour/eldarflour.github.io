The consumption of animal products is a practice intertwined with human history, culture, and health. However, it is worth knowing the origin of these products, particularly in the modern era where the production techniques tend to come as a surprise, and raise questions about whether these products can be considered healthy, despite the presence of various nutrients.

The Dominion documentary summarises the topic. It does, however, contain many graphic scenes of animal abuse.

Besides that, the land requirement for meat is steep. If everyone in Britain obtained their B12 from beef, while otherwise eating a basic diet, then there would be land for around 7 million people, or 21 million for lamb, 56 million for factory-farmed pork, or 29 million for home-raised chicken.

With a population of around 65 million people, it seems fair to say that meat is not a viable source of B12 for Britain as a whole, at least not when self-sufficiency is considered and factory-farms are avoided.
