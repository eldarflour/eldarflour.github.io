Duckweed is potentially a good source of vitamin B12, and it has the additional benefit of being able to replace the leaves, due to its high vitamin K content, as well as the vegetables if two or three times more are added.

It can also be grown in a small aquarium.

However, it’s not commonly available, and not due to a lack of supply. As with chlorella, it may be useful to look at duckweed as a way to make natural vitamin B12 and K supplements.

B12 content may rely on a non-sterile growing environment. It is then also worth highlighting that it’s usually washed in hot water, before being dried. At this point in time though, not enough seems to be known about the B12 content of duckweed and the environmental factors required for its presence.
