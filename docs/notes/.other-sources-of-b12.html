It was historically common to use compost from human manure in agriculture. Individuals using ‘night soil’ to grow their crops were found to have normal levels of B12 in their blood, despite a nearly-vegan diet. This is not entirely unexpected, given the high levels of the bacteria that produce B12 in the later stages of the human digestive system. It may be worth noting that ordinary dirt is not a very concentrated source. Otherwise, some may prefer a tablet.
